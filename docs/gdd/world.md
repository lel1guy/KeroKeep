# World

## The Kingdom of Batrachia

Batrachia is a civilization of humanoid frogs — bipedal, armored, wielding staves and magic like any medieval realm. They are not comedic or cartoonish; they are warriors holding the last wall of a dying kingdom.

The kingdom has fallen piece by piece. Kero Keep is the final fortress. Behind its walls: the last farmers, scavengers, and mages of Batrachia. Beyond them: nothing but the enemy.

## Kero Keep — The Last Fortress

The keep sits at the edge of a swamp — the ancestral homeland of the Batrachian people. Its walls are old stone, patched with scrap metal. Four lanes run from the swamp toward the wall, carved by centuries of siege traffic.

**At the top:** the swamp. Monsters spawn here and march downward.

**The wall:** four mage positions. Each mage fires magic bolts at approaching enemies. The wall has been reinforced, scarred, rebuilt — it tells the story of every siege it's survived.

**Below the wall:** the heart of the keep. A storeroom filled with salvaged scrap. A farm where crops push through the muck. A dormitory for survivors. A workshop where engineering happens.

## The Mages of the Wall

The keep's ranged defenders are mages — Batrachian spellcasters who channel swamp energy into magic bolts. Each mage is a milestone earned through resources and combat gates.

| Mage | Unlock | Additional Gate |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **1st** | 50 Gold | Tutorial — free after first 10 kills |
| **2nd** | 150 Gold + 30 Scrap | Kill 50 monsters |
| **3rd** | 400 Gold + 100 Scrap + 20 Food | Survive 5 waves |
| **4th** | 1000 Gold + 300 Scrap + 50 Food | Defeat first boss OR reach wave 15 |

Each mage is recruited through a ceremony — a named frog takes position on the wall with a short one-liner intro. The names are drawn from a pool of Batrachian names (Kael, Lyra, Torvin, Sera, Brann, Vexa, Oric, Myra). The fourth mage should *hurt* to earn — the moment players remember.

## The Enemy

Four monster types, each forcing different tactical responses:

| Monster | Stage | HP | Speed | Threat | Character |
|---------|-------|----|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Skeleton** | 0 | Low | Fast | Low | Basic rush — no special mechanics |
| **Zombie** | 1 | Medium | Slow | Medium | Tanky — takes more hits to bring down |
| **Orc** | 2 | High | Medium | High | Armored — reduces click damage, magic bolts pierce |
| **Demon** | 3 | Very High | Fast | Very High | Teleports, fire AoE, unique attack patterns |

Each stage introduces new mechanical challenges beyond bigger numbers. Orcs require magic. Demons require positioning.

## Bosses

Bosses break the rhythm of endless waves. Two types:

| Type | Spawn | Threat | Reward |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Random** | % chance per wave, increases with stage | 5× HP, 3× damage, unique attack pattern | Gold, Essence |
| **Quest** | Summoned via quest completion | Named, 10× HP, unique mechanics, phases | Prestige currency, survivor trait, engineering recipe |

Random bosses keep the siege tense. Quest bosses give structure and clear progression gates.

## Tone

Grounded fantasy, not comedy. The frog identity adds visual distinction and personality, but the stakes are real. Survivors can starve. Walls can fall. The siege never ends. Think *Redwall* grit — bravery, loss, resilience — with an amphibian skin.
