# Concept & Vision

## The High Concept

> Kero Keep is an incremental siege defense game where armored frog warriors hold the last wall of a dying kingdom against an endless horde. Click to kill. Earn resources. Build. Survive. The siege never ends — but neither do you.

## Genre

- **Primary:** Incremental idle (numbers go up, dopamine hits)
- **Secondary:** Tower defense (archers on the wall, waves of enemies)
- **Tertiary:** RPG elements (named survivors, skill progression, bosses)

## The Fantasy

Most idle games feel like spreadsheets — numbers climb but you don't *see* anything happening. Kero Keep makes the world react:

- Archers you see fire from the wall
- Waves you feel bearing down
- Builds you watch evolve visually as you progress
- Survivors you name, level, and grow attached to
- A keep that changes — banners appear, walls reinforce, scars mark past cycles

## Design Pillars

### P1 — EARNED GROWTH

Progress traces back to player action — mobs killed, archers commanded, resources chosen and spent. Clicking and timers are pacing mechanisms, not idle automation. The game doesn't grow without you.

### P2 — PERSISTENT SIEGE

There is no pause. No victory screen. The siege is eternal. The world runs even while you're away — random hordes and bosses appear. Offline persistence is a reward for progress, not a given from the start.

### P3 — FAIR FOREVER

No pay-to-win. No premium currency. Money never touches game balance. If monetization ever happens, it's optional — cosmetics or temporary boosts that anyone can earn. The game respects your time and your wallet equally. Non-negotiable.

## The Feeling

| Moment | Feeling |
|--------|---------|
| **Growth beat** | Watching your base harden, numbers climb, survivors grow stronger — knowing *you* earned every increment through action |
| **Siege tension** | The dread before a wave. "Will we make it?" Relief when the last enemy falls. Pressure of knowing it *never* ends |
| **Boss encounter** | A named threat appears. Music shifts. The screen darkens. This one matters — beating it means real progression |
| **Prestige reset** | The wall has held through everything. Time to begin again — stronger, wiser, with scars that show. A bittersweet goodbye to the survivors who got you here |

## Target Audience

- Fans of incremental/idle games who want more visual feedback
- Tower defense players who enjoy resource management
- Players who value fair monetization (no P2W)
- Anyone who likes seeing their progress reflected in the world

## Why Frogs?

Because fantasy is oversaturated with humans, elves, and dwarves. Batrachia — a kingdom of armored, bipedal frog warriors — is visually distinct, memorable, and opens design space that generic fantasy doesn't. Think *Redwall* grit with an amphibian skin. Not comedic. Not cartoonish. Warriors holding the last wall of a dying kingdom.
